        Consider the set of integers 2, 3 ... 99. After removal of the first two primes (2, 3) we are left with 96 members, 48 of which are divisible by 2, leaving 48, of which a further 16 are divisible by 3. The remaining 32 plus the members 2 & 3 leave a maximum of 34 potential primes less than 100. Therefore, at least 134 primes <1000 are greater than 100. The sum of these must exceed 13,400 therefore option (A) is false.

        Of the 168 total, 167 of these are odd numbers and sum to an odd number. Therefore options (C) & (D) are both false.

        Only option (B) survives.
